The Journey Begins: Green Coffee

Green coffee refers to unroasted coffee beans, the raw seeds of the coffee cherry. Ethiopia's diverse microclimates produce beans with distinctive flavor profiles based on altitude, soil, and variety.

Did you know? Green coffee is rich in antioxidants and is the starting point for all coffee flavors. Its quality is influenced by altitude, soil, and post-harvest processing.
Roasted Coffee
Transformation Roast Profiles

The Art of Roasting

Roasting transforms green coffee beans, developing their flavors and aromas. Ethiopian beans can be roasted to various levels—light roasts preserve floral notes, while dark roasts highlight chocolate and spice notes.

Roasting Tip: The roast level affects acidity, body, and aroma. Experimenting with different roast profiles brings out unique characteristics in Ethiopian beans.
Brewing Methods
Preparation Extraction

From Bean to Cup

Ethiopian coffee shines through various brewing methods. Traditional ceremonies use a clay pot called a jebena, while modern methods include pour-over, espresso, and cold brew—each revealing different aspects of Ethiopia's complex coffee profiles.

Brewing Insight: The grind size and brewing method impact the final cup. Explore different techniques to experience the full spectrum of Ethiopian coffee flavors.
